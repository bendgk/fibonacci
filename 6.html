How The Process Of Psychotherapy Works

Dr. Doulova Psychiatrist
Sep 16, 2019 3:43 PM
  • #care
  • #doctor
  • #wecare

In a nutshell, the process of psychotherapy would help a person by giving him some counselling in order to cure hindrances in their mental health that would affect their daily routines. Of course this has to be done over the course of time simply because it is quite a tedious process that will take a lot of time and energy to do. So just to give an idea on how this works, here are some bits of information on this subject matter.

Now the first thing that one should take note of would be the time frame of this type of therapy. Always remember that the time frame would depend on the severity of the mental illness of the patient because if a patient has a severe case, then more sessions are needed. It works conversely for those with less severe cases who would need a smaller number of sessions as compared to those who have very deep rooted problems from their past.

One of the important aspects of this type of therapy would be the behavioral therapy course. Now this type of therapy is for people to do when they are either at work or if they are in the comfort of their own home. This is actually done by those who have a lot of fear and anxiety issues or for those who cannot seem to relax.

Basically, this therapy includes a lot of breathing exercises. Believe it or not, the way a human breathes is directly proportional to the level of anxiety that the person is actually feeling in that moment. It also involves a lot of visualization exercises that will help him calm the mind.

Now there is of course the identification or diagnosis portion of the therapy. Identifying the main problem of the issue can be quite a challenge because most issues are stemmed from the past. This actually means that a lot of issues are deep rooted problems that need plucking out.

This portion of the therapy is actually done in the office because it involves the psychologist trying to dig out information from the client. If the client is feeling depressed, then there has to be a reason for this kind of phenomenon. It is up to the psychologist to let the client bring it out.

Of course the main goal of this therapy is to allow the client to feel like the burden is actually off his shoulders. When the client has that kind of feeling, then the therapy is actually a success. This therapy is actually used for those who are depressed but cannot determine exactly why.

So basically, this is just an overview of what the process of psychotherapy is like and will help give beginners an idea of what is actually going on in the office. There are actually more steps and more processes in psychotherapy that the psychologist would have to do. Although there are still many things to take note of, these steps are the most basic ones that cannot be skipped.
