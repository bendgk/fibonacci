The Benefits Of Receiving Psychotherapy Treatment

Dr. Doulova Psychiatrist
Nov 1, 2019 2:13 PM
  • #counseling
  • #help
  • #care

Psychotherapy is a form of therapy that involves verbal communication between a trained mental health professional and an individual, couple, or group of people. The nature of the discussions may focus on the triggers, influences, and causes of various psychological disorders as well as possible solutions. Individuals who visit a psychotherapist do so to increase their sense of well-being.

The process is recommended for individual who are struggling to cope with problems related to work, home, or personal relationships.  Patients are encouraged to share their behaviors, thoughts, and feelings.  Discussions focus on helping them to identify constructive ways to deal with problems.  In some cases, the patient is seeking support while going through a stressful situation such as a divorce or a change in career.

Psychotherapy is goal-oriented and helps patients develop effective problem solving skills. The first step typically involves an initial consultation with the psychotherapist to determine which changes the patient wants to make. The chances for success are greater if the goals are reasonably attainable.

Some patients will pursue a treatment course that includes both therapy and medication. Prescription medications are often used to treat mental disorders with a biological component.

There are several types of therapy, and it can be separated into a categories:

Behavior therapy focuses on educating patients about how behavior changes can have an effect on emotional state of mind. Patients can begin by keeping a log of daily activities and looking for ways to change behavior that will result in positive experiences. This may include scheduling activities that are socially reinforcing. Role playing is an effective technique that is used to improve the patient's social interaction skills. The patient also learns to anticipate awkward situations and adapt to them in a socially acceptable way.

Cognitive therapy recognizes that the way individuals think influences the way they feel. System of wrong beliefs can lead to depression and other psychological issues.  Individuals must learn on how to become aware when thinking is inaccurate and respond to challenges in an effective way. The therapist's role is to guide the patient on how to view a situation in an alternative ways to improve individual's well being.

Group therapy involves one therapist and as many as a dozen patients with related psychological problems. Patients benefit from a group format because they receive feedback from other participants and have the opportunity to observe their actions and hear responses. Having the benefit of receiving different perspectives promotes change and growth.

Group therapy offers additional benefits to participants. Patients are able to learn new coping methods by observing how others come up with solutions for similar problems. Interacting with a group is an effective way to improve social skills and learn how to communicate with others. These skills translate into better interactions with individuals outside the group.

Psychotherapy is helpful in treating various forms of anxiety, depression, phobia, etc.. It can also be helpful for individuals who are dealing with conflict but have not been diagnosed with a mental illness. A medical professional can determine if psychotherapy alone is enough or if combination with prescription medication will lead to a better result.
