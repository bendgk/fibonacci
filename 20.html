A Brief Insight Into Psychotherapy

Dr. Doulova Psychiatrist
Jan 16, 2019 5:39 PM
  • #help
  • #care
  • #doctor

Psychotherapy is comprised of an array of different techniques. After knowing their client's psychological problem, a good therapist will adjust the treatment to break down the person's resistance. This will help them make the changes necessary for the patients to recover. Psychotherapists use different procedures on their patients to complete the treatment.

Psychotherapy is a counseling technique used to aid mentally ill patients overcome stumbling blocks on their way to recovery. Several sessions are normally required for recovery. For one to benefit from this procedure, they have to open up to the doctor.

Though there's no definite time frame, about twenty sessions are necessary to deal with issues such as panic disorders, obsessions and phobias. If the process doesn't work, additional techniques such as medication and behavioral therapy may be necessary. Behavior therapy is a component of psychotherapy which involves exercises such as visualization, breathing and relaxation. Fears and phobias can be gradually faced in order to enable the client face the inhibitors.

This procedure is considered functional when one is able to wake up in the morning and function normally without any lethargic feelings. Instead of feeling insane, one feels evenly balanced, normal and happy. Feelings that were otherwise problematic can now be faced without difficulty.

There are many people who suffer from some sort of depressive disorder. This condition affects an individual's mental, physical and emotional well-being. This process has models designed to address the underlying causes of depressions and provide amicable solutions.

Symptoms of depressions are normally reinforced by thinking patterns. Many people feel depressed due to the difficulty in facing the actual reasons why they can't function normally. An individual's perception of the world affects their life experiences. Psychotherapy is used to identify thinking patterns and see how they affect a person's life. There are various processes used in this approach. Depression treatment requires short term therapy for about six months and is normally applied for individuals whose condition ranges from mild to moderate.

Cognitive-behavioral therapy is one of the processes used and which is comprised of different treatment methods. Each technique views thought processes as the trigger of emotional pain. This kind of therapy treats the symptoms by examining thought processes and substituting them with constructive thought patterns. This is a time-intensive procedure where an end point is decided by both the patient and the therapist at the beginning.

There's also interpersonal therapy which focuses on an individual's relationship with other people in his life. Here, unconscious motivations and childhood experiences are discussed to understand how a person's lifestyle influences their symptoms. This procedure utilizes several techniques within the process.

Psychodynamic therapy is an intense approach to psychotherapy. Its main focus is on psychic tensions and unconscious motivations. Here, the relationship between the patient and the therapist are very important. Symptoms are treated by helping one identify negative emotional patterns that developed in their childhood and how they influence current relationships. This technique is designed to get rid of emotional blocks which hold the symptoms in place.

Psychotherapy may not be enough for cases of severe depression. In such situations, therapy has to be combined with antidepressants. These help to stabilize an individual's emotional state to help them comprehend and apply fresh thinking habits.
